Glossary & Help

Key terms for posting and debate structure.

Debate Topic
The central resolution being debated. Must be set before posting and framed neutrally to include both sides' contested areas.
Argument Unit (AU)
A structured contribution consisting of: (1) factual premises (AU_FACTS) and (2) an inference/conclusion (AU_INFERENCE). Traceable to source spans.
Canonical FACT Node
A deduplicated, normalized atomic fact extracted from posts. Assigned P(true) through fact-checking. Forms the basis of Factuality scores.
Canonical ARGUMENT Node
A deduplicated argument structure linking supporting facts to an inference. Forms the basis of Reasoning and Coverage scores.
Coverage
How well your side addresses opposing arguments. Addressing high-leverage opposing arguments improves this score.
Modulation
The moderation process that evaluates posts against visible rules. Only Allowed posts influence scoring.

For full specification, see About page.
