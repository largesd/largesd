Glossary & Help

Key terms and metrics used throughout the system.

CI(D)
Confidence Interval of D (the difference score). If this interval crosses zero, the verdict is "NO VERDICT" because FOR and AGAINST are statistically indistinguishable.
D = FOR − AGAINST
The margin between overall FOR and AGAINST scores. Positive = FOR leading; Negative = AGAINST leading.
Ft,s (Factuality)
Mean truth probability across canonical FACT nodes for topic t, side s. Calculated as average of P(fact true).
Reasont,s (Reasoning)
Median reasoning strength across canonical ARGUMENT nodes for topic t, side s. Scored by independent judges.
Covt,s (Coverage)
How well side s addresses the opposing side's arguments, weighted by argument leverage. Higher = better rebuttal coverage.
Qt,s (Quality)
Geometric mean of Factuality, Reasoning, and Coverage. Balances all three components without tuned weights.
Relt (Relevance)
Topic relevance weight based on claim-producing content mass. Determines how much each topic contributes to the overall score.
Steelman
A summary that presents the strongest version of each side's arguments, not a strawman. Generated from canonical arguments.
IQR
Interquartile Range — shows spread of judge scores. Wider IQR = more disagreement among evaluators.
Drift Score
Measures how much a topic has changed from its previous snapshot. Higher = more change.
Canonical FACT/ARGUMENT
Deduplicated, normalized representations of claims and arguments extracted from posts. Used for consistent scoring.
