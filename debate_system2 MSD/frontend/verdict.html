Glossary & Help

Key terms and metrics used throughout the system.

D (Margin)
Difference between overall FOR and AGAINST scores: D = Overall_FOR − Overall_AGAINST. Positive = FOR leading; Negative = AGAINST leading.
CI(D)
Confidence Interval for D. If CI(D) crosses zero (includes both positive and negative values), the verdict is "NO VERDICT".
Ft,s (Factuality)
Mean probability that canonical FACT nodes are true. Calculated as average of P(fact true) across all facts for topic t, side s.
Reasont,s (Reasoning)
Median reasoning strength of canonical ARGUMENT nodes, scored by independent judges. Higher = better structured arguments.
Covt,s (Coverage)
Proportion of opposing arguments that are addressed (rebutted/qualified/conceded), weighted by argument leverage. Higher = better rebuttal coverage.
Qt,s (Quality)
Geometric mean of Factuality, Reasoning, and Coverage: (F × Reason × Cov)^(1/3). Enforces balance without tuned weights.
IQR
Interquartile Range (75th percentile − 25th percentile). Measures spread of judge scores. Wider IQR = more disagreement among evaluators.
