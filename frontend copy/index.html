Glossary and Help

Key terms used across snapshot, topic, and verdict pages.

CI(D)

Confidence interval of D (difference score). If the interval crosses zero, verdict remains NO VERDICT.

D = FOR - AGAINST

Margin between overall FOR and AGAINST scores. Positive values favor FOR; negative values favor AGAINST.

F_t,s (Factuality)

Average truth probability across canonical FACT nodes for each topic and side.

Reason_t,s (Reasoning)

Median reasoning strength across canonical ARGUMENT nodes for each topic and side.

Cov_t,s (Coverage)

How well one side addresses opposing arguments, weighted by leverage.

Q_t,s (Quality)

Geometric mean of factuality, reasoning, and coverage for balanced scoring.

Rel_t (Relevance)

Topic relevance weight derived from claim-producing content mass.

Steelman

Strongest representation of each side based on canonical arguments, not weakest points.

IQR

Interquartile range showing spread of judge scores; wider means less evaluator agreement.

Drift score

Degree of topic change relative to previous snapshot.

Canonical FACT and ARGUMENT

Normalized, deduplicated claim and argument units used for stable scoring.
