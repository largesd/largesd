# Product

## Register

product

## Users
General public users with mixed technical backgrounds who participate in, evaluate, or monitor debates. Core roles include debaters, judges, admins, and observers. Users need to orient quickly, understand what to do next, and complete key actions with low friction.

## Product Purpose
Provide a clear, trustworthy interface for running blind, evidence-based debate workflows from setup to verdict. The product exists to make reasoning quality, factual grounding, and procedural fairness legible without requiring expert technical knowledge.

Success means users can:
- start and configure debates confidently,
- submit and evaluate arguments without confusion,
- understand outcomes and audit trails at a glance.

## Brand Personality
Rigorous, calm, institutional.

The interface should project trust, procedural fairness, and seriousness without feeling intimidating. Language and visuals should feel measured, neutral, and clear rather than promotional or flashy.

## Anti-references
- Social-feed styling that introduces status signals or engagement gamification (likes, popularity badges, influencer-style profiles).
- Trend-heavy "vibecoded" aesthetics that prioritize novelty over clarity (neon glows, decorative gradients, excessive visual effects).
- Overly playful or entertainment-first visual language that weakens perceived procedural integrity.

## Design Principles
1. Navigation first: the next meaningful action should be obvious within one glance.
2. Procedural trust over spectacle: interfaces should foreground evidence, reasoning, and state, not decoration.
3. Clarity under complexity: progressively disclose detail so novice users are not overwhelmed.
4. Consistent role-aware flows: debaters, judges, admins, and observers should each get predictable, purpose-fit paths.
5. Plain language before notation: outcomes must be interpretable without reading mathematical symbols. Technical details belong in expandable sections.
6. Composed interaction rhythm: motion and feedback should support comprehension while preserving a calm tone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Accessibility target: WCAG 2.1 AA (assumed baseline unless you specify a stricter target).
- Use standard motion only where it improves understanding, with full reduced-motion support.
- Preserve strong contrast, clear labels, and keyboard-visible focus across all critical interactions.
